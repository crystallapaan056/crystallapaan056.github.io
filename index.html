Overview of the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR)

The Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) is a unique and culturally rich region located in the northern part of the Philippines. Known for its vast mountain ranges, the region is home to indigenous peoples who have preserved their cultural heritage for centuries. The region is composed of six provinces: Abra, Apayao, Benguet, Ifugao, Kalinga, and Mountain Province, with Baguio City as its summer capital and an important hub for tourism, education, and culture.

The Cordillera region is celebrated for its picturesque rice terraces, including the famous Banaue Rice Terraces, which are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its people, the Igorots, have a deep connection to nature, reflected in their traditional practices, colorful festivals, vibrant weaving, and indigenous culinary arts.

Visitors to CAR can explore a rich tapestry of cultural traditions, from the intricate weaving of textiles to the stunning landscapes of mountain villages and cultural landmarks. The region is also known for its hospitality, with a strong emphasis on community and ancestral knowledge passed down through generations.

Abra

Culture & Traditions

Abra is known for its vibrant Tingguian weaving, bamboo crafts, and rich cultural heritage. The province celebrates various festivals such as the Arya! Abrenian Kawayan Festival, highlighting its bamboo industry.

Festivals: Abrenian Kawayan Festival, Arya Abra (March)

Cuisine: Miki Abra (noodle soup), Masa Podrida (shortbread cookies), Goloria (doughnuts), Inladit (sweet crackers), Pinalatan (chicken with pomelo leaves), Pinaltit (ground-pork appetizer), Abraw (vegetable stew), Kiniing/Etag (smoked meat), Pinunnog (smoked sausage)

Tourist Spots: Cassamata Hill National Park, Pinsal Falls, Angalo & Aran Legend

Apayao

Culture & Traditions

Apayao is known for its rich tradition of basket weaving, oral rituals, and agricultural practices. The Say-am Festival in February is an important cultural celebration.

Festivals: Say-am Festival (February)

Cuisine: Binungor (spicy stew), rice-based dishes, herbal teas, Sinursur (smoked fish), Tupig (rice cake), Patupat (rice cake), Sagket (fermented fish sauce)

Tourist Spots: Lussok Crystal Cave, Calanasan Waterfalls, Apayao River, Agamata National Park

Benguet

Culture & Traditions

Benguet, with Baguio as its capital, thrives in its agricultural heritage, highlighted by the famous Panagbenga Festival. Its arts, indigenous practices, and the legacy of the Igorot tribes are integral parts of its culture.

Festivals: Panagbenga Festival (February), Adivay Festival (November)

Cuisine: Pinikpikan (ritual chicken soup), Pinuneg (blood sausage), Inabraw (vegetable stew), Gipah (herbal tea), Benguet coffee, Pancit Habhab (Baguio style)

Tourist Spots: Mount Pulag, Mines View Park, Burnham Park, Tam-Awan Village, BenCab Museum

Ifugao

Culture & Traditions

Ifugao culture revolves around rice, and the world-famous Banaue Rice Terraces are a testament to this heritage. The Hudhud chants, performed during important occasions, reflect their rich cultural heritage.

Festivals: Imbayah Festival (April), Gotad Festival (June)

Cuisine: Pinikpikan (traditional chicken dish), Baya (rice wine), Rice cakes, Root crops

Tourist Spots: Banaue Rice Terraces, Batad Rice Terraces, Ifugao Museum

Kalinga

Culture & Traditions

Kalinga is known for its traditional tattooing culture, particularly the art of Apo Whang-Od. The Bodong (peace pact) system is a unique custom that remains alive in this province.

Festivals: Matagoan Festival (June), Ullalim Festival (February)

Cuisine: Kalinga coffee, Smoked meat, Mountain rice, Tubers

Tourist Spots: Buscalan Village (tattoo by Apo Whang-Od), Chico River, Sleeping Beauty Mountain

Mountain Province

Culture & Traditions

The Mountain Province is home to various tribes like the Bontoc and Kankanaey. It is famous for the Lang-ay Festival and the hanging coffins of Sagada, a unique tradition that draws visitors from all over the world.

Festivals: Lang-ay Festival (April)

Cuisine: Pinikpikan, Etag (smoked meat), Inabraw (vegetable stew), Homemade yogurt, Tapuy (rice wine)

Tourist Spots: Sagada Hanging Coffins, Sumaguing and Lumiang Caves, Bontoc Museum
